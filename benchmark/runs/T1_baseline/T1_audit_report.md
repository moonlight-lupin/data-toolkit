## 3. Column-by-column changes

**Ref** — carried through unchanged; used as the row identifier.

**Payment date** — source mixed three date formats (`DD/MM/YYYY`, `YYYY-MM-DD`, `D Mon YYYY`). All three were parsed and re-expressed uniformly as `DD MMM YYYY` (e.g. `01 Mar 2026`). The day-of-month sequence across consecutive rows (1, 2, 3, 4 …) was checked to confirm slash-dates are day-first (`DD/MM/YYYY`) and not US month-first — e.g. source row 21 `16/04/2026` can only be 16 Apr (a month value of "16" is invalid), which rules out `MM/DD`. No dates were unparseable.

**Payer** — leading/trailing whitespace trimmed. 8 of 40 rows had stray trailing double-spaces in the source (e.g. `"Bramley & Cole LLP  "`, `"Silverbirch Capital  "`); names themselves were not otherwise altered. Affected refs: P-1001, P-1006, P-1011, P-1016, P-1021, P-1026, P-1031, P-1036.

**Country** — standardised to one value per country. Source had 4 variant spellings collapsing to 3 countries:

| Standardised value | Source variants seen (count in raw data) |
|---|---|
| United Kingdom | `UK` (11), `U.K.` (5), `United Kingdom` (5) |
| United States | `USA` (5), `U.S.A.` (5) |
| Singapore | `Singapore` (5), `SINGAPORE` (6) |

**Amount** — thousands-separator commas removed and values converted to numeric. Two source cells carried a literal `£` prefix (rows 12 and 29, refs P-1008 and P-1023) with the Currency column left blank; per the rule "£ means GBP", these were parsed as numeric amounts with Currency set to GBP. Two cells could not be safely parsed and were **not guessed**:

| Ref | Source line | Source value | Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| P-1012 | 16 | `pending` | Not a number — kept as raw text `"pending"` in the Amount cell, flagged |
| P-1027 | 33 | *(blank)* | Missing — left blank, flagged |

**Currency** — carried through as given (GBP/USD), except the two `£`-prefixed rows above where GBP was inferred from the £ symbol as instructed.

**Flag** (added column) — plain-language note on any row that needed manual attention; blank for all rows with no issue. Flagged rows are also highlighted in pale yellow in the workbook.
